Technical Interview

I had recently just taken my technical interview on Skilled and boy was it an eye-opening experience. I was given this opportunity to do a practice technical interview after graduating. I definitely felt nervous because it was testing my ability to code and what I had learn in Flatiron. Prior to my interview, I was trying to study all the fundamentals of Javascript that I can. I even looked up a Skilled interview experience just so I can prepare myself on what I was about to do. I realized that you can't always prepare yourself for what's to come. You can only prepare yourself to an extent.

So I started the test and I met the instructor that was going to do my technical interview. We went straight into coding, there was a problem that needed to be solve with Javascript. My task was to camel-case a JSON string's keys and it was in kebab-case. I had 45 minutes to complete this task and I still didn't feel that was adequate time for me to complete. I didn't really know what to say during the process, but I received feedback that I should be talking during this process. Just so the interviewer knows what my thought process is like. I felt like I was all over the place and couldn't gather my thoughts on this task. I was trying to individually camelcase each key rather than making the code applicable to multiple objects. I also had trouble with recognizing how to break down the JSON string into separate parts. I couldn't finish solving the problem, but I did receive assistance from the interviewer.

The feedback that I've received is being able to speak out my process, breaking down the problem into smaller pieces, and getting my fundamentals down. Overall, I will study my fundamentals and learn more about recursion of Javascript. I'm eager to get better, but I know it takes time. Practice makes perfect.
